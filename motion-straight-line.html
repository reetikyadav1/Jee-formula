Motion in a Straight Line – JEE Practice

1. Displacement is:

Solution: Displacement is the vector difference between final and initial positions.

2. Average velocity is:

Solution: Average velocity = total displacement / total time.

3. In uniform motion, acceleration is:

Solution: Uniform motion means constant velocity, so acceleration = 0.

4. The slope of x-t graph gives:

Solution: Slope of x-t graph = dx/dt = velocity.

5. Instantaneous velocity is:

Solution: Instantaneous velocity = lim (Δx/Δt) as Δt → 0.

6. In non-uniform motion, velocity:

Solution: Non-uniform motion means changing velocity.

7. The area under v-t graph gives:

Solution: Area under v-t graph = ∫v dt = displacement.

8. Acceleration is the rate of change of:

Solution: Acceleration = dv/dt.

9. For uniform acceleration, v-t graph is:

Solution: v = u + at, so straight line with slope = a.

10. Distance can be:

Solution: Distance is scalar, always positive or zero.

11. The slope of a-t graph gives:

Solution: Slope of a-t graph = da/dt = jerk.

12. In uniform velocity, acceleration is:

Solution: Uniform velocity means no change in velocity, so a = 0.

13. Position is a:

Solution: Position is a vector (has magnitude and direction).

14. For motion with constant acceleration, x-t graph is:

Solution: x = ut + (1/2)at², so parabola.
