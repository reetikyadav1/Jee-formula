Units & Dimensions – JEE Practice

1. The SI unit of force is:

Solution: Force is mass × acceleration, so unit is kg·m/s², called Newton.

2. Dimensional formula of energy is:

Solution: Energy = (1/2)mv², so [M L² T⁻²].

3. Which of the following is a fundamental unit?

Solution: Kilogram is a fundamental unit (mass).

4. The dimensional formula of pressure is:

Solution: Pressure = Force/Area = [M L T⁻²]/[L²] = [M L⁻¹ T⁻²].

5. In dimensional analysis, the principle of homogeneity states:

Solution: Dimensions of all terms in an equation must be identical.

6. The number of significant figures in 0.00123 is:

Solution: Leading zeros are not significant; 1,2,3 are significant, so 3.

7. Which equation is dimensionally correct? (v=velocity, t=time)

Solution: s = vt has [L] = [L T⁻¹][T], correct.

8. The unit of coefficient of viscosity is:

Solution: Viscosity η = F/(A dv/dx), unit Pa·s.

9. In error analysis, absolute error is:

Solution: Absolute error is the difference Δx.

10. Convert 1 km/h to m/s:

Solution: 1 km/h = 1000m / 3600s = 5/18 ≈ 0.278 m/s.

11. Dimensional formula of Planck's constant is:

Solution: h = E/f, E = [M L² T⁻²], f = [T⁻¹], so [M L² T⁻¹].

12. The least count of a vernier caliper is:

Solution: Typically 0.01 mm for standard vernier.

13. Which is not a fundamental quantity?

Solution: Velocity is derived (length/time).
