System of Particles & Rotational Motion – JEE Practice

1. Centre of mass of a system is the point where:

Solution: CM is where entire mass acts for translational motion.

2. Torque τ is:

Solution: τ = r × F, vector product.

3. Angular momentum L =:

Solution: L = r × p = I ω.

4. Moment of inertia I for a ring is:

Solution: I = MR² for thin ring.

5. Rotational kinetic energy K_rot =:

Solution: K_rot = (1/2) I ω².

6. In rolling without slipping, v_cm =:

Solution: v_cm = ω R for pure rolling.

7. Conservation of angular momentum applies when:

Solution: τ_net = 0 implies L constant.

8. Parallel axis theorem: I = I_cm +:

Solution: I = I_cm + M d².

9. For a solid sphere, I_cm =:

Solution: I_cm = (2/5) MR².

10. Torque due to force F at distance r is:

Solution: τ = r F sinθ.

11. Centre of mass for two particles is at:

Solution: r_cm = (m1 r1 + m2 r2)/(m1 + m2).

12. Angular acceleration α =:

Solution: α = dω/dt.

13. In pure rolling, friction is:

Solution: Static friction provides torque without slipping.

14. Rotational inertia depends on:

Solution: I depends on mass and axis.
