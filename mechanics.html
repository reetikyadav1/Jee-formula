Thermodynamics – JEE Practice

1. In an isothermal process, the internal energy of an ideal gas:

Solution: For an ideal gas, internal energy depends only on temperature. In an isothermal process, temperature is constant, so internal energy remains constant.

2. The work done in an adiabatic process is equal to:

Solution: In an adiabatic process, Q = 0, so from the first law (ΔU = Q - W), W = -ΔU.

3. For a Carnot engine operating between temperatures T1 and T2 (T1 > T2), the efficiency is:

Solution: Efficiency η = 1 - T2/T1 for a Carnot engine.

4. In a PV diagram, an isothermal process is represented by:

Solution: For an ideal gas, PV = constant for isothermal, which is a hyperbola.

5. The first law of thermodynamics is a statement of:

Solution: The first law states that energy is conserved: ΔU = Q - W.

6. For an adiabatic expansion of an ideal gas, the temperature:

Solution: In adiabatic expansion, work is done by the gas, so internal energy decreases, leading to a drop in temperature.

7. The efficiency of a heat engine is given by:

Solution: Efficiency η = W/Q_in, where W is work output and Q_in is heat input.

8. In a PV diagram, the area under the curve represents:

Solution: The area under the PV curve equals the work done on/by the system.

9. For a monatomic ideal gas, the internal energy U is:

Solution: For monatomic gas, U = (3/2) nRT.

10. In an isobaric process, the work done is:

Solution: Work done W = PΔV for isobaric process.

11. The maximum efficiency of a heat engine is given by the Carnot cycle when:

Solution: Carnot efficiency is maximum for reversible operation.

12. For an adiabatic process, the relation between P and V is:

Solution: For adiabatic, TV^{γ-1} = constant, leading to PV^γ = constant.

13. The work done by the system in a cyclic process is:

Solution: In a cyclic process, ΔU = 0, so W = Q_net, and it's the area enclosed in the PV diagram.
