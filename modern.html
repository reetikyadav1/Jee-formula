Modern Physics – JEE Practice

1. The photoelectric effect demonstrates:

Solution: Photoelectric effect shows light behaves as photons (particles).

2. In Bohr's model, the radius of nth orbit is proportional to:

Solution: r_n ∝ n².

3. The half-life of a radioactive substance is:

Solution: Half-life is the time for half the initial atoms to decay.

4. The binding energy of a nucleus is:

Solution: Binding energy is the energy needed to disassemble the nucleus.

5. In semiconductors, doping with pentavalent impurity creates:

Solution: Pentavalent atoms donate electrons, creating n-type semiconductor.

6. The stopping potential in photoelectric effect depends on:

Solution: V_s = (hν - φ)/e, depends on frequency (or wavelength).

7. Rutherford's experiment showed that:

Solution: Most alpha particles passed through, some deflected, indicating a small nucleus.

8. The decay constant λ is related to half-life T½ by:

Solution: T½ = ln2 / λ.

9. In nuclear fission, the mass defect is converted to:

Solution: Mass defect releases energy as kinetic and heat.

10. The energy of an electron in nth orbit in Bohr model is:

Solution: E_n = -13.6 / n² eV.

11. Alpha particles are:

Solution: Alpha particles are ^4_2He nuclei.

12. In p-n junction diode, forward bias:

Solution: Forward bias reduces depletion layer, allowing current.

13. The work function φ of a metal is:

Solution: φ is the minimum energy needed to eject an electron.

14. Nuclear fusion occurs at:

Solution: Fusion requires overcoming Coulomb repulsion, needing high T.
