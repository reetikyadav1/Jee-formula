Laws of Motion – JEE Practice

1. Newton's first law states that:

Solution: Law of inertia: no net force means no change in motion.

2. The SI unit of force is:

Solution: F = ma, unit kg·m/s² = Newton.

3. In a free body diagram, forces are shown as:

Solution: Forces are vectors with magnitude and direction.

4. Static friction acts when:

Solution: Static friction prevents motion when at rest.

5. Newton's third law is:

Solution: Every action has an equal and opposite reaction.

6. Kinetic friction is:

Solution: Kinetic friction is less than maximum static friction.

7. Pseudo force acts in:

Solution: Pseudo force appears in accelerating frames.

8. The coefficient of friction is:

Solution: μ = limiting friction / normal force.

9. In an elevator accelerating upward, apparent weight:

Solution: Pseudo force downward, so N = mg + ma, weight increases.

10. Newton's second law is:

Solution: Net force causes acceleration.

11. Limiting friction is:

Solution: Just before motion starts.

12. In a lift accelerating downward, weight feels:

Solution: Pseudo force upward, N = mg - ma, lighter.

13. Friction opposes:

Solution: Friction opposes relative motion.

14. In inertial frame, pseudo force is:

Solution: No pseudo force in inertial frames.
