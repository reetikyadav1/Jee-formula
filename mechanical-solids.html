Mechanical Properties of Solids – JEE Practice

1. Stress is defined as:

Solution: Stress = F/A.

2. Strain is:

Solution: Strain = ΔL/L.

3. Young's modulus Y =:

Solution: Y = stress / strain.

4. Bulk modulus K =:

Solution: K = -ΔP / (ΔV/V).

5. Shear modulus η =:

Solution: η = stress / shear strain.

6. In the elastic limit, stress is:

Solution: Hooke's law: stress ∝ strain.

7. Poisson's ratio σ =:

Solution: σ = - (lateral strain) / (longitudinal strain).

8. The yield point is:

Solution: Beyond yield point, deformation is permanent.

9. Ductile materials have:

Solution: Ductile materials deform plastically before breaking.

10. Breaking stress is:

Solution: Breaking stress is ultimate tensile strength.

11. For steel, Young's modulus is:

Solution: Y ≈ 2×10^11 Pa for steel.

12. In compression, strain is:

Solution: Strain is negative for compression.

13. The proportionality limit is:

Solution: Stress ∝ strain up to proportionality limit.

14. For rubber, Young's modulus is:

Solution: Rubber has low Y, high elasticity.
