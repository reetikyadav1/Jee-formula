Motion in a Plane – JEE Practice

1. A vector has:

Solution: Vectors have both magnitude and direction.

2. The resultant of two vectors A and B is minimum when:

Solution: R = √(A² + B² + 2AB cosθ), minimum at θ = 180°.

3. In projectile motion, horizontal velocity:

Solution: No horizontal acceleration, so constant.

4. Relative velocity of A w.r.t B is:

Solution: V_AB = V_A - V_B.

5. Time of flight in projectile motion is:

Solution: T = 2u sinθ / g.

6. In circular motion, centripetal acceleration is:

Solution: a_c = v² / r towards center.

7. The angle for maximum range in projectile is:

Solution: R_max at θ = 45°.

8. Vector addition follows:

Solution: Both laws apply for vector addition.

9. In uniform circular motion, speed is:

Solution: Speed constant, velocity changes due to direction.

10. The horizontal range R =:

Solution: R = (u² sin2θ) / g.

11. A scalar has:

Solution: Scalars have magnitude only.

12. In projectile, maximum height H =:

Solution: H = (u² sin²θ) / (2g).

13. Relative velocity in 1D is:

Solution: V_AB = V_A - V_B, vector.

14. Angular velocity ω in circular motion is:

Solution: ω = v / r.
