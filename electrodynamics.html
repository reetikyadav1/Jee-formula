Electrodynamics – JEE Practice

1. The electric field due to a point charge q at a distance r is:

Solution: E = kq/r² for a point charge.

2. The capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor is:

Solution: C = ε₀A/d for parallel plates.

3. Ohm's law states that V is proportional to:

Solution: V = IR, so V ∝ I.

4. The force on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field is:

Solution: F = BIL sinθ.

5. Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction states:

Solution: Induced emf ε = -dΦ_B/dt.

6. In AC circuits, the rms value of current is:

Solution: I_rms = I₀/√2 for sinusoidal AC.

7. Gauss's law relates electric flux to:

Solution: ∫E·dA = q_enclosed/ε₀.

8. The energy stored in a capacitor is:

Solution: U = (1/2) CV² = (1/2) QV = Q²/2C.

9. Kirchhoff's first law is based on:

Solution: Junction rule: ΣI_in = ΣI_out.

10. The magnetic field inside a solenoid is:

Solution: B = μ₀nI for infinite solenoid.

11. Lenz's law is a consequence of:

Solution: Induced current opposes the change, conserving energy.

12. In series AC circuit, impedance Z is:

Solution: Z = √(R² + (X_L - X_C)²).

13. Coulomb's law is applicable for:

Solution: F = kq1q2/r² for point charges; integrable for distributions.

14. The equivalent capacitance of two capacitors in series is:

Solution: 1/C_eq = 1/C1 + 1/C2.
