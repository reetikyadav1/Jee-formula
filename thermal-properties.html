Thermal Properties of Matter – JEE Practice

1. Coefficient of linear expansion α =:

Solution: α = (ΔL / L) / ΔT.

2. Coefficient of volume expansion γ =:

Solution: γ = 3α for isotropic solids.

3. In calorimetry, heat lost =:

Solution: Heat lost by hot body = heat gained by cold body.

4. Specific heat capacity c =:

Solution: c = Q / (m ΔT).

5. Conduction heat transfer Q =:

Solution: Q = (k A ΔT t) / L.

6. Radiation heat transfer Q =:

Solution: Q = σ A ε (T⁴ - T0⁴).

7. Convection is heat transfer by:

Solution: Convection involves bulk movement of fluid.

8. Thermal conductivity k is:

Solution: Metals have high k due to free electrons.

9. Stefan-Boltzmann constant σ =:

Solution: σ = 5.67×10^-8 W/m²K⁴.

10. Heat capacity C =:

Solution: C = m c.

11. Wien's displacement law λ_max T =:

Solution: λ_max T = 2.9×10^-3 mK.

12. Latent heat of fusion is:

Solution: L_f = Q / m for phase change.

13. Emissivity ε =:

Solution: ε = 1 for perfect blackbody.

14. Thermal expansion of liquids is measured using:

Solution: Dilatometer measures volume change.
